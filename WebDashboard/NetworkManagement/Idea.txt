Wholesale Network Dashboard Proposal: Intelligent Purchase Optimization & Demand-Supply Balance
Executive Summary
This dashboard transforms your wholesale network from reactive stocking to data-driven procurement. It identifies exactly which product categories to reduce (loss-making, slow-moving, congestion-creating) and which to amplify (profit-drivers, high-turnover, popularity-builders). Each objective ties to specific chart types and analytical models.

Core Dashboard Architecture (4 Interlocking Panels)
Panel 1: Expense Minimization & Loss Prevention
Objective: Identify categories to minimize purchase to avoid losses, holding costs, and dead stock.

Metric / Analysis	Chart Type	Why This Works	Action Trigger
Inventory Turnover Ratio by Category	Horizontal Bar Chart (sorted low→high)	Quickly spot categories with <2 turns/year—capital stuck in slow movers	Minimize purchase for bottom 20% categories
Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)	Heatmap (categories vs. months)	Reveals seasonal stagnation; red zones = cash traps	Flag categories consistently >90 DIO
Gross Margin Return on Inventory Investment (GMROII)	Scatter Plot (GMROII vs. Turnover)	Bottom-left quadrant = low profit + slow sales → prime reduction candidates	Set automated "reduce purchase" alert
Shrinkage & Obsolescence %	Bullet Graph (actual vs. benchmark)	Identifies categories with expiry, damage, or theft above 3%	Immediate purchase freeze if >5%
Carrying Cost % of Revenue by Category	Stacked Column Chart (storage + finance + risk)	Reveals hidden costs—high storage density ≠ high profit	Renegotiate supplier terms or delist
Analysis Model Used: ABC-XYZ Matrix

Classify A (high revenue), B (medium), C (low) combined with X (stable demand), Y (seasonal/trendy), Z (erratic)

C-Z items = immediate minimization candidates

Panel 2: Profit & Popularity Maximization
Objective: Identify categories to increase purchase—high profit, fast turnover, customer magnets.

Metric / Analysis	Chart Type	Why This Works	Action Trigger
Category Contribution Margin	Waterfall Chart (category by category)	Visually shows which categories truly fund your business after variable costs	Increase purchase for top 5 contributors
Sales Velocity (units/day)	Funnel Chart (broad category → subcategory → SKU)	Drills down to fastest-selling items; popularity proxy	Reorder trigger when velocity exceeds 80% of safety stock
Customer Walk-in Driver (what brings people)	Word Cloud / Bar Chart (from POS + survey data)	Bestsellers ≠ profit drivers; this shows loss leaders that attract crowds	Keep these in stock even if thin margin
Cross-Selling Index by Category	Network Graph (nodes = categories, edges = co-purchase frequency)	Identifies which categories, when bought together, boost basket size	Increase purchase of companion categories
New Customer Acquisition by Category	Stacked Area Chart (time series)	Categories that consistently bring new buyers → long-term growth	Promote + increase stock depth
Analysis Model Used: RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) + Market Basket Analysis

Find categories purchased by high-RFM customers

Apriori algorithm identifies product associations (e.g., buying cement → likely buy sand)

Panel 3: Congestion Management & Supply-Demand Balance
Objective: Avoid warehouse congestion, stockouts, and bullwhip effect across your wholesale network.

Metric / Analysis	Chart Type	Why This Works	Action Trigger
Storage Utilization by Category	Treemap (area = cubic storage occupied, color = turnover rate)	Instantly spot "space hogs" that sell slowly → congestion culprits	Reduce purchase or renegotiate storage allocation
Order Fulfillment Lead Time Variability	Control Chart (X-bar R chart)	Suppliers with high lead time variance force you to overstock → congestion	Shift purchase to reliable suppliers even at higher unit cost
Days of Supply (DOS) Distribution	Histogram with overlaid normal curve	Shows if 80% of categories have DOS > ideal range (e.g., >60 days) → systemic overbuying	Set DOS target band (14–45 days for wholesale)
Stockout Rate vs. Overstock Rate	Quadrant Chart (4 boxes)	Top-left quadrant (high stockout + high overstock) = forecasting failure → chaos	Implement demand-smoothing for those categories
Supplier Reliability Score by Category	Gauge Chart + Trend Line	Tracks on-time delivery % + quality rejection %	Shift purchase from unreliable to reliable sources
Network-Wide Inventory Days	Sankey Diagram (inflow → storage → outflow)	Visualizes where inventory gets stuck between shops in your network	Redistribute from congested shops to fast-moving ones
Analysis Model Used: Seasonal Decomposition (STL) + Safety Stock Optimization

Separate trend, seasonal, residual components

Set dynamic safety stock = Z-score × MAD of forecast error × lead time

Reduces congestion by eliminating blanket overstocking

Panel 4: Demand Forecasting & Automated Rebalancing
Objective: Proactive, not reactive—predict demand and reallocate inventory across your shop network.

Metric / Analysis	Chart Type	Why This Works	Action Trigger
Forecast vs. Actual Demand (MAPE) by Category	Fan Chart (prediction intervals)	Shows which categories have widening error bands → need human review	Automated PO reduction for categories with MAPE >30%
Lead Time Demand Distribution	Probability Density Curve	If 95th percentile lead time demand > current stock → risk of stockout AND congestion from overprotection	Calculate optimal reorder point = μ(LTD) + SS
Inter-Shop Transfer Opportunity	Chord Diagram (shops as nodes, transfer volume as arcs)	Identifies surplus in Shop A, deficit in Shop B → buy less overall	Internal transfer before external purchase
Promotional Lift Decay Curve	Exponential decay line chart	Shows how long a promotion's effect lasts before demand returns to baseline	Time purchases to coincide with lift period, not after
What-If Scenario Sliders	Interactive controls (price, lead time, demand shock)	Simulate: "If I reduce Category X by 30%, how does congestion change?"	Test decisions before committing capital
Analysis Model Used: Croston’s Method (for intermittent demand) + Dynamic Programming for Multi-Echelon Inventory

For wholesale, demand is often lumpy—Croston outperforms simple exponential smoothing

Multi-echelon optimization reduces total network inventory by 15–25%

Data Sources Required
Data Source	Fields Needed	Update Frequency
POS System	Category, SKU, qty sold, price, discount, shop ID, date	Daily
Purchase Orders	Supplier, lead time, unit cost, qty ordered, delivery date	Daily
Inventory System	On-hand qty, storage location, holding cost per cubic meter, expiry date	Real-time
Warehouse Mgmt	Bin utilization, putaway/picking time per category	Daily
Customer Data (if any)	Repeat customer flag, first purchase date, basket contents	Weekly
Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to Track Dashboard Success
Metric	Formula	Target
Cash-to-Cash Cycle	DIO + DSO - DPO	<45 days
Inventory Turnover	COGS / Avg Inventory	>6x/year for wholesale
Perfect Order Rate	% orders on time, complete, damage-free	>95%
Gross Margin ROI	Gross Profit / Average Inventory Cost	>3.0
Storage Cost per Revenue	Total storage cost / Category revenue	<8%
